# CS-250-8-2-Journal

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

It’s all about finding out what actually brings the client value. User stories are amazing because they break a massive project into bite-sized pieces. Instead of stressing over the whole app, we can just build one thing at a time—like a flight search—during a quick two-week sprint.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I definitely prefer a flexible Agile approach since project requirements change so fast. Moving forward, I want to keep using Kanban boards to stay visually organized. I'll also use sprint planning to protect against scope creep, ensuring sudden changes don't ruin our timeline.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

It means sharing the load in a self-organizing group instead of waiting for a manager's orders. It’s really about honest communication. In our Daily Scrums, admitting you were stuck on something—like migrating files—meant we could fix it together right away instead of waiting for a status email.
